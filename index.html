About Us

At Holland Neurotech Inc., we are driven by the vision to pioneer advanced technologies that bridge advancements in neurotech with everyday health management. Our commitment is to leverage cutting-edge science to create solutions that enhance longevity and optimize women's health through practical, data-driven insights and innovative wearable technologies.

What is Neurotech?

Neurotechnology encompasses a broad range of technological solutions designed to understand, enhance, and interface with the nervous system. At Holland Neurotech Inc., we harness this technology to develop wearables that not only track but actively improve women's health and promote longevity.

Our applications of neurotech go beyond simple health monitoring. They integrate deep insights from brain and body interactions to offer personalized health management strategies tailored to women's unique physiological needs across different life stages. This includes innovative approaches to managing menstrual health, pregnancy, and menopause, ensuring that our users can achieve optimal health outcomes.

By blending cutting-edge research in neurotech with practical applications in health and wellness, we aim to revolutionize how women interact with technology to manage their health, enhance well-being, and extend healthy lifespan.

Our Philosophy

When I was studying Clinical Neuroscience, it occurred to me that many neurological disorders are incurable but can have their likelihood significantly reduced with appropriate preventative health practices. This is maximised for women's health, where current health systems already bias towards men in our understanding of symptoms, diagnostics, and treatments. Holland Neurotech aims to focus on these areas often overlooked and underserved by mainstream technology.

As many in health have experienced, this sent me down a rabbit hole of learning and experimenting with different tech. Frustrated by the lack of systems that met my needs or the needs of women like me, I realised that the tools we use must evolve. They can't just gather data- they need to also have female-specific algorithms, and communicate the data relevant for women in ways that are actionable and simple to understand.
